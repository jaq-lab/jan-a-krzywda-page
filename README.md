## üéØ Mission Statement

Our research organization is dedicated to advancing quantum technologies through innovative approaches that combine quantum physics, machine learning, and control theory. We focus on solving fundamental challenges in quantum computing by developing practical tools, algorithms, and systems that bridge the gap between theoretical understanding and real-world implementation.

**Key Objectives:**
- **Democratize Quantum Technology**: Make quantum concepts accessible through education and interactive tools
- **Accelerate Quantum Development**: Create digital twins and simulation platforms for faster quantum system development
- **Enable Robust Quantum Control**: Develop real-time feedback systems for noise mitigation and parameter optimization
- **Advance Quantum Machine Learning**: Explore the intersection of quantum computing and artificial intelligence
- **Build Practical Quantum Systems**: Translate theoretical advances into working quantum devices and applications

## üî¨ Research Areas

### üß† [Quantum Learning Machines](https://github.com/jaq-lab/quantum-learning-machines)
**Advanced quantum machine learning and reservoir computing systems**

*GitHub Pages: [Explore Research](https://jaq-lab.github.io/quantum-learning-machines/)*

Practical use of noisy quantum devices for data-driven learning, reinforcement learning, and generative AI. Our research explores quantum reservoir computing, generative quantum models, and noise-resilient quantum learning algorithms.

### ‚ö° [Charge Noise in Spin Qubits](https://github.com/jaq-lab/charge-noise-spin-qubits)
**Experimental characterization and ab-initio simulation of charge noise**

*GitHub Pages: [Explore Research](https://jaq-lab.github.io/charge-noise-spin-qubits/)*

Arguably the biggest obstacle to large-scale spin qubit devices. We focus on comprehensive noise characterization, spatial correlation analysis, and mitigation strategies using dynamical decoupling and optimal control.

### üéÆ [Quantum Games](https://github.com/jaq-lab/quantum-games)
**Interactive quantum games driving progress in quantum technologies**

*GitHub Pages: [Explore Research](https://jaq-lab.github.io/quantum-games/)*

Games have historically driven progress across many fields. We're creating interactive quantum games that democratize quantum education, accelerate research through crowdsourcing, and build community around quantum technologies.

### üî¨ [Spin Qubits Emulator](https://github.com/jaq-lab/spin-qubit-emulator)
**Digital twin development for spin qubit systems**

*GitHub Pages: [Explore Research](https://jaq-lab.github.io/spin-qubit-emulator/)*

Spin qubits are complex systems requiring extensive data for autonomous tuning and algorithm development. Our comprehensive digital twin platform enables advanced simulation, analysis, and optimization of spin qubit devices.

### üéõÔ∏è [Quantum Feedback Control](https://github.com/jaq-lab/quantum-feedback-control)
**Real-time FPGA control for tracking and correcting quantum fluctuations**

*GitHub Pages: [Explore Research](https://jaq-lab.github.io/quantum-feedback-control/)*

Quantum systems suffer from dynamical noise that degrades performance. We deploy real-life FPGA controllers to track and correct random fluctuations, enabling robust quantum operations in realistic environments.

### ü§ñ [Machine Learning for Quantum Systems](https://github.com/jaq-lab/machine-learning-quantum)
**Classical machine learning methods for processing and simulating quantum systems**

*GitHub Pages: [Explore Research](https://jaq-lab.github.io/machine-learning-quantum/)*

Classical machine learning methods for processing and simulating quantum systems from sparse data. We develop intelligent calibration protocols, real-time parameter tracking, and physics-informed learning approaches.
